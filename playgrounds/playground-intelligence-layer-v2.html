Tier Classification
The first fork in the road. Routes clients into the right onboarding path and unlocks the appropriate tools. This is deterministic — no AI needed.
Trace a Path
Scenario: Pre-revenue startup, $0 current, targeting $2M in 2 years
1
Input: Current revenue $0, target $2M, "SaaS marketplace for pet services"
2
Problem: $0 revenue falls below Sub-$1M tier minimum. No tier handles pre-revenue.
3
Result: Client gets routed to Sub-$1M by default. Market Sizer has no baseline to work from. Revenue Forecaster can't compute growth multiple (divide by zero).
!
Gap: Pre-revenue is Valerie's most common intake profile. Without a dedicated path, these clients will hit a dead end in the first 2 minutes. Need a "pre-revenue" tier with different assumptions.
This is the kind of gap that kills adoption. The decision logic is the front door — if it doesn't handle the most common persona, everything downstream is irrelevant. Fix this before building AI sub-agents.

TAM/SAM/SOM Synthesis
The hero capability. Takes "I sell HR software to mid-market" and produces a sourced, defensible market sizing with penetration analysis. This is where AI earns its keep.
See in Action
Input: "HR compliance software, mid-market (500-5K employees), US"
TAM$14.2B — US HR compliance software, all segments sourced
SAM$2.4B — Mid-market, US-only computed
SOM$48M — Based on GTM capacity projected
Plausibility$8M target = 0.33% SAM. Plausible. 6.7x growth exceeds 5.2x median. review
ComparablesRippling 2.1% SAM at Series C. Gusto 1.8% at similar stage.
~
Risk: No human validation gate between AI forecast and client delivery. If the AI confidently produces a wrong TAM (hallucinated benchmark), the client builds their business plan on it. Consider a consultant review step before the client sees final numbers.
+
Opportunity: Historical accuracy tracking. After 20+ clients, you could show: "Our TAM estimates have been within 15% of actual market reports." That's a trust-building flywheel — and a competitive moat for the platform.
Structure Advisor could be rules-based instead of AI. Entity type + jurisdiction matching is a decision matrix, not a creative task. Downgrading it from AI sub-agent to a rules tool would reduce complexity without losing value — and free up engineering for the capabilities that actually need AI.
